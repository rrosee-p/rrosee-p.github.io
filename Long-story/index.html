The Last Orchard

A story about memory, land, and the things we choose to keep.

Scroll to begin ↓

The Orchard

My family’s orchard sat at the end of a dirt road, where the earth smelled sweet and the trees bent gently in the wind. My grandfather used to say the land remembered us.

Seasons of Work

We learned to prune in winter, plant in spring, harvest in fall. Each season taught a lesson. Each tree held a story. Every year, we grew alongside them.

The Threat

Then came the letter. The company wanted the land. Promised profit. Promised efficiency. Promised progress. But the orchard was not something that could be replaced.

The Choice

I stayed. I learned. I listened to the wind move through the trees. The orchard is smaller now, but it is still ours. Some things are worth tending — even when it’s hard.
