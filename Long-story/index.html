The Last Orchard

A story about memory, land, and the things we choose to keep.

The Orchard

My family’s orchard sat at the end of a dirt road, where apple trees stretched for rows and rows. The air smelled of blossoms in spring.

Seasons of Work

We pruned branches in winter, planted in spring, and harvested fruit in fall. Each season taught patience, care, and pride in what we tended.

The Threat

One day a letter arrived. A company wanted the land. Promises of profit and “efficiency” were made, but we knew the orchard’s worth could not be measured in dollars.

The Choice

We chose to stay. We tended the orchard, smaller but still alive. Some things are worth holding onto, even when the world tries to move past them.
