The Last Orchard

A story about land, memory, and what we choose to keep.

The Orchard

My family’s orchard sat at the end of a dusty road. Every spring, apple blossoms filled the air with a sweetness we thought would last forever.

Learning the Work

We pruned branches in winter, planted in spring, and gathered fruit when the air began to cool. Year after year, we grew up with the trees.

The Letter

One day, a letter came. A company wanted the land. They said they could make it “useful.” But we knew the orchard already had a purpose.

The Choice

We stayed. We kept tending the trees. Some things are worth holding onto, even when the world tries to move past them.
