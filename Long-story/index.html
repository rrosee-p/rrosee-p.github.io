Red Riding Hood

A young girl is sent by her mother to bring food to her sick grandmother. She is told to stay on the path and not talk to strangers.

In the forest, she meets a wolf who asks where she’s going. She tells him about her grandmother’s house. The wolf secretly plans to get there first.

The wolf arrives at the grandmother’s house, gets inside, and disguises himself as the grandmother, waiting for Little Red Riding Hood.

She enters and notices something strange about “Grandma” — “What big eyes you have!” — until the wolf reveals himself and tries to eat her.

A woodcutter or hunter hears the commotion, saves Little Red Riding Hood and her grandmother, and she learns to never trust strangers and to follow directions.
