The Last Orchard

A story about memory, land, and what we choose to keep.

The Orchard

My family’s orchard sat at the end of a dusty road. Apple trees stretched in rows, blooming early every spring. The air always smelled like blossoms and warm soil.

Seasons of Work

We learned to prune branches in winter and gather apples in the fall. Every season asked for patience. Every tree felt like an old friend.

The Letter

One day, a letter arrived. A company wanted the land. They said the orchard was “outdated.” They promised something “more modern.” But some things can’t be replaced.

The Choice

We chose to keep the orchard. It’s smaller now, and harder to maintain. But when the wind moves through the leaves, it sounds like every memory is still alive.
